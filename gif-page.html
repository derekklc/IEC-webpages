BACKGROUND INFORMATION

The IEC Global Impact Fund(GIF) provides grants for projects that address specific global environmental, governance and societal challenges through the application of IEC International Standards and Conformity Assessment Systems.



The UN has recognized electricity as the cornerstone for economic development. Electrical, electronic and information technologies, which are core to IEC work, have the potential to positively impact environmental, societal and governance(ESG) challenges. When countries adopt IEC work in standardization and conformity assessment, they are better able to build up national quality infrastructure, achieving greater efficiencies while improving the safety of products, workers, people, and the environment.


Companies that use IEC work can access global best practices to build quality devices and systems that are valued beyond national borders. The IEC Global Fund aims to bring the benefits of IEC International Standards and the IEC Conformity Assessment Systems to find solutions to some of the most pressing challenges facing the world.


IEC Global Impact Fund projects target specific countries where effective, safe, and efficient solutions based on IEC technical know-how can be implemented by small and medium enterprises (SMEs). Through the IEC national contact, the SME can also garner broad support among national stakeholders. While projects generally target one specific country, they are designed so that they can be replicated in other countries or scaled up regionally to maximize impact.


EXHIBITION

IEC Promotion Center Exhibition Hall covers an area of 950 m². The theme of the exhibition hall centers around the IEC's remarkable development throughout modern civilization, the journey of electro-technical standards development, and China's participation and contribution to international standardization progress. With a modern technological design and interactive presentation, the exhibition tour shows our guests how IEC standards benefit multiple aspects of our daily life, which embodies the IEC's vision of "IEC everywhere for a safer and more efficient world".
